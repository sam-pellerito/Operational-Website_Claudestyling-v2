Operational Impact Calculator

See how operational savings can fund customer growth, increase revenue, and improve blended margin when reinvested into marketing — or compare keeping savings versus reinvesting them into growth.

Use this to justify an efficiency project, show the value of cost savings, or decide how to allocate a one-time savings windfall.

Built by Sam Pellerito

How to use this tool

  1. Enter your current revenue, gross margin, AOV, and CPA to set a baseline.
  2. Enter the one-time operational savings — from a freight renegotiation, co-man switch, or process improvement.
  3. Choose how much of the savings to reinvest into marketing and set your expected ROAS.
  4. Review the effect on revenue, new customers, CPA, contribution, and gross margin.
  5. Compare scenarios — keep savings, reinvest all, or split — to make the best decision.

Business inputs

Savings & reinvestment inputs

ROAS = revenue return, not profit return. Example: 3.0 means every $1 of ad spend generates $3 of revenue. Typical CPG ROAS ranges 2–6× depending on channel.
Use gross margin for your current business, but a lower margin here for new revenue — after COGS, fulfillment, discounts, and transaction fees tied directly to this campaign. Usually lower than your headline gross margin.

Metric Before After Change

Incremental CPA (AOV ÷ mROI) shows the implied cost per new customer from this campaign. If it is lower than your current CPA, the reinvestment is acquiring customers more efficiently than your existing average.
