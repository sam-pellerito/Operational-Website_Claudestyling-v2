Cash-to-Cash Cycle

See how long your cash is tied up between paying for inventory, selling product, and collecting from customers. The cash conversion cycle (DIO + DSO − DPO) is the single biggest driver of working capital for CPG brands.

Use this to understand working capital pressure, compare customer or supplier terms, and estimate how much cash you can free up by turning inventory faster, collecting sooner, or negotiating longer supplier terms.

The three components

For best results, use average balances over the year, not just today's ending balance. A single day's balance can distort the result if the business is seasonal or a large invoice just hit.

📦 DIO

Days Inventory Outstanding — how long product sits before it's sold.

= Avg Inventory ÷ COGS × 365

🧾 DSO

Days Sales Outstanding — how long before customers pay you.

= AR ÷ Revenue × 365

🤝 DPO

Days Payable Outstanding — how long before you pay your suppliers.

= AP ÷ COGS × 365

Working capital freed = (days improved ÷ 365) × annual revenue. Annual savings = freed WC × capital cost %. Working capital tied up estimates the cash required to fund the operating cycle; financing cost estimates the annual cost of carrying that cash at your cost of capital.

How to improve each metric

📦 Reduce DIO (faster inventory turns)

  • Reduce slow-moving or obsolete SKUs
  • Improve demand planning accuracy
  • Negotiate lower MOQs with suppliers
  • Shorten supplier lead times
  • Increase sell-through with promotions

🧾 Reduce DSO (collect faster)

  • Tighten payment terms with retailers
  • Invoice immediately on shipment
  • Offer early-pay discounts
  • Reduce retailer deduction delays
  • Follow up systematically on overdue invoices

🤝 Extend DPO (pay suppliers later)

  • Negotiate net-30 → net-45 or net-60 terms
  • Align payment timing with customer collections
  • Use supplier financing programs carefully
  • Move from prepaid to net terms as you grow
  • Consolidate suppliers for stronger leverage

Component breakdown

The cash conversion cycle equals the total bar length minus DPO. A shorter cycle means less cash trapped in operations.
